BornoBit — Brand Guide

Restaurant ERP · visual identity reference

The BornoBit mark is a confident single-stroke (monoline) B on a warm orange badge, with one gold “bit” spark at the corner — a nod to the digital Bit in the name. Use these assets and tokens consistently across the staff console, customer site, documents and decks.

Note: the application UI currently themes teal via the --bo-* tokens. This guide defines the new orange brand. Re-theming the app to orange is an optional, separate step — the logo assets below stand on their own.

Badge gradient runs #FB923C → #EA580C (top→bottom). The “bit” spark is gold #FFD27A — the only non-orange accent. The B glyph is always white on the badge.

Minimum size & clear space

Keep clear space around the mark equal to the badge’s corner radius on every side. Don’t crowd it.

Do & don’t

✓Use the full-color logomark on light or neutral surfaces.
✓Use the mono mark on photos, dark UI, or single-color print.
✓Scale proportionally; keep the rounded-square badge and the white B.
✕Don’t recolor the B or change the stroke weight of the monoline.
✕Don’t stretch, rotate, or add shadows/outlines to the badge.
✕Don’t move or multiply the gold spark, or swap it for another color.

Typography

Brand & UI font is Inter (fallback Segoe UI, system-ui). The wordmark sets “BornoBit” at weight 700, letter-spacing −0.01em, with “Bit” in Orange 600.
